Learn about Islam

Five Pillars of Islam

1. Shahādah — Testimony of Faith

Affirming that there is no deity worthy of worship except Allah, and that Muhammad ﷺ is His Messenger.

2. Ṣalāh — Prayer

Five daily prayers at set times: Fajr, Ẓuhr, ʿAṣr, Maghrib, and ʿIshā'.

3. Zakāh — Almsgiving

A due on eligible wealth to purify it and support those in need.

4. Ṣawm — Fasting in Ramaḍān

Abstaining from food, drink, and marital relations from dawn to sunset with devotion and patience.

5. Ḥajj — Pilgrimage

The sacred pilgrimage to Makkah once in a lifetime for those who are able.

Core Sources & Concepts

  • Qur'an: The final revelation in Arabic, preserved and recited by Muslims worldwide.
  • Hadith & Sunnah: Prophetic teachings and practices explaining the Qur'an's guidance.
  • ʿAqīdah: The creed outlining belief in Allah, angels, scriptures, prophets, the Last Day, and divine decree.
  • Adab & Akhlāq: Etiquette and character—kindness, honesty, patience, and mercy.
